1 Setup and Method

This report helps us find where retail corridor opportunity is most promising in the Jacksonville market. We move from broad market context to tract-level screening, then prioritize areas for deeper site review.

The output is practical:

  • A shortlist of high-opportunity geographies
  • A clear explanation of why those places rank higher
  • A repeatable framework we can apply in other metros

1.1 How to Read This Notebook

Think of the process in three steps:

  1. Market context: We start with a quick check of Jacksonville’s growth, housing pressure, and peer position.
  2. Tract screening and ranking: We evaluate tracts using a consistent scorecard to identify where demand momentum and development headroom are strongest.
  3. Action-oriented prioritization: We carry top-performing areas into zone and parcel analysis to support field diligence and underwriting.

1.2 Key Assumptions (Business View)

This model is designed for screening, not final investment decisions. It is meant to narrow the market to better starting points for human review.

Important assumptions:

  • We compare tracts within the same metro for fairness.
  • We prioritize growth and housing pipeline signals.
  • We treat pricing pressure as a guardrail, not the only decision factor.
  • Final decisions still require local market diligence (tenants, zoning, access, competition).
Method Transparency (for technical readers)
  • Data sources include ACS, BPS, tract geometry, and parcel attributes from validated section artifacts.
  • Metrics are standardized within the target metro to support apples-to-apples tract comparison.
  • The score combines weighted components aligned to the strategy thesis: growth, housing pipeline, development headroom, price pressure, and commute context.
  • Gates are applied to enforce strategy fit and reduce false positives.
  • Validation reports are generated by section and surfaced in the appendix.
  • This is a screening model. Site-level diligence and underwriting are still required.

3 Section 3 Eligibility and Scoring

This section ranks every tract first, then applies strategy gates so we can focus on the strongest candidates.

What we do:

  • Build one tract score across all tracts using growth, supply, headroom, price pressure, commute exposure, and income.
  • Use the top 25% of scored tracts as the clustering cutoff universe.
  • Keep gates as secondary diagnostics for strategy-fit storytelling.
  • Surface the top tracts with plain-language why tags.

What this means:

  • Higher-ranked tracts are stronger candidates for zone and parcel follow-up.
  • Lower-ranked tracts are deprioritized, not permanently excluded from future review.
  • The model provides a transparent starting point, not a final verdict.

4 Zone Systems

This section turns the Section 3 top-scoring tract set into operating zones for field review and corridor prioritization.

How zones are built:

  1. Start from the top 25% of tracts by Section 3 tract score (cluster seed universe).
  2. Project tract centroids and connect nearby tracts using a fixed distance threshold.
  3. Form connected components, then assign small/noise groups to the nearest core cluster.
  4. Dissolve tract groups into cluster zone polygons and compute zone summary metrics.

5 Retail Overlay and Shortlist

Section 05 translates high-opportunity zones into a practical parcel shortlist for broker and field review.

How the shortlist score works: - Zone quality (50%): stronger cluster zones score higher based on upstream tract performance. - Local retail context (25%): parcels in tracts with stronger existing retail intensity score higher. - Parcel characteristics (25%): favors larger parcels, lower value-per-square-foot, and more recent sales activity.

Value and sales logic: - JV (Just Value) is the primary value signal. - We compute value intensity as JV / land square feet and winsorize extremes for stability. - SALE_PRC1 is retained for sales context and recency (via last_sale_date), not as the primary value field.
